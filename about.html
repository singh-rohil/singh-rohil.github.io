About Me

My path to computer science wasn't linear. It started in a high school health sciences academy and led through molecular biology research at Rutgers, where I first discovered my love for working with data. Analyzing DNA sequences and publishing findings to the NIH database showed me how computational tools can solve problems that matter.

Today, I combine that analytical background with software engineering to build solutions that make a difference. Whether I'm training AI models at Outlier, analyzing database modernization timelines at Viscosity, or mentoring students as a Teaching Assistant at Northeastern, I bring the same curiosity and attention to detail to everything I create.

As a 3x National Champion in SkillsUSA Quiz Bowl and an Oracle Certified Java Programmer, I thrive on challenges that require both technical skill and quick thinking. I'm currently pursuing my Computer Science degree at Northeastern University, where I'm deepening my expertise in object-oriented design, databases, and software architecture.
